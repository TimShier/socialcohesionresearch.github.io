Understanding our Data

  • Population size 2015 (UN figures drawn from Statistics Times)
  • The country’s ranking in terms of population size in Africa
  • The number of tweets in 2015 generated from that country
  • The Volume Index – the country’s volume of tweets expressed as a deviation from the continental mean. This is an absolute volume index that disregards population size.
    • Here, a score of 100 is the mean line. Scores above the mean indicate that the country tweets more than the average. Scores below the mean indicate that they tweet less than the average.
    • So a country with a Volume Index of 230 tweets 2.3 times as much as the continental average. A country with a Volume Index of 60 tweets 0.6 as much as the continental average.
  • The country’s ranking in terms of volume of tweets in Africa
  • The percentage of tweets in 2015 that were in English
  • The percentage of tweets in 2015 that were in Arabic
  • The percentage of tweets in 2015 that were in French
  • The percentage of tweets in 2015 that were in Portuguese
  • The percentage of tweets in 2015 that were in Indigenous or other languages
  • The Tweets Per Person (TPP) per annum score - the number of tweets per year sent by each member of the population

  • The Twitter Activity Index Score – this is an expression of the TPP per annum score as a deviation from the continental mean. Unlike the Volume Index score, this takes population size into account:
    • A score of 100 is the mean line. Scores above the mean indicate that the country tweets more than the average per member of the population. Scores below the mean indicate that they tweet less than the average per member of the population.
    • For example, a country with a Twitter Activity Index Score of 430 tweets per person at a rate 4.3 times that of the continental average. In contrast, a country with a Twitter Activity Index score of 30 tweets at a rate per person 0.3 times as much as the continental average.
  • The Twitter Activity Index ranking - Ranks countries on their Twitter Activity Index Score.

We intend to track changes in the Index over time, and produce work on the growth of social media over time – with a focus on African social media. Episodic analytic reports will be drawn from the Twitter Activity Index, and will be available on this website. If you would like updates on this work as it unfolds, please contact us here.
