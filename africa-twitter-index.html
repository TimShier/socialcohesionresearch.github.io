Africa Twitter Index

Understanding African Twitter Usage

The African Twitter Activity Index provides accurate metrics on Twitter activity in all African countries. We consider this an indication of the level of general social media use in a given country.

The Index provides the level of Twitter Activity in a given country in relation to African Twitter activity. The same model will be repeated with other continental indices - it is necessary to compare apples with apples given the vast discrepancies between levels of social media use in the developing vs the developed world.

Through intelligent use of Crimson Hexagon, we are able to gauge and analyse twitter activity in all countries in the world (excluding China, where it is restricted) with excellent accuracy from 2014 onwards. We caution that this data is accurate to a confidence level of 95%.

The Index allows us to show our clients exactly how much social media activity exists in any country, and assists us in offering a scoping service that allows our clients a look at the volume of relevant traffic on any topic from both local and global perspectives before committing to a study.

This is the first time that a Twitter Index has been constructed, and we are proud to call it our own.

It aims to answer questions such as:
  • What is a large amount of Twitter traffic in a given African country?
  • What percentage of those Tweets were in English/other languages?
  • How has traffic grown over the last few years?

It is an ongoing project and will in time deliver a variety of continental and regional indices as a well as a Global Twitter Activity Index

The 2015 African Twitter Activity Index is below, and for each country we provide the following information:

- Population size 2015 (UN figures drawn from Statistics Times)
  • The country’s ranking in terms of population size in Africa
  • The number of tweets in 2015 generated from that country

The Volume Index

the country’s volume of tweets expressed as a deviation from the continental mean. This is an absolute volume index that disregards population size.

  • Here, a score of 1 is the mean line. Scores above the mean indicate that the country tweets more than the average. Scores below the mean indicate that they tweet less than the average.
  • So a country with a Volume Index of 2.3 tweets 2.3 times as much as the continental average. A country with a Volume Index of 60 tweets 0.6 as much as the continental average.
  • The country’s ranking in terms of volume of tweets in Africa

    • Language

    • The percentage of tweets in 2015 that were in English
    • The percentage of tweets in 2015 that were in Arabic
    • The percentage of tweets in 2015 that were in French
    • The percentage of tweets in 2015 that were in Portuguese
    • The percentage of tweets in 2015 that were in Indigenous or other languages

Tweets per Person

  • The Tweets Per Person (TPP) per annum score - the number of tweets per year sent by each member of the population

Index and Index Ranking

The Twitter Activity Index Score –this is an expression of the TPP per annum score as a deviation from the continental mean. Unlike the Volume Index score, this takes population size into account:

  • A score of 1 is the mean line. Scores above the mean indicate that the country tweets more than the average per member of the population. Scores below the mean indicate that they tweet less than the average per member of the population.
  • For example, a country with a Twitter Activity Index Score of 4.3 tweets per person at a rate 4.3 times that of the continental average. In contrast, a country with a Twitter Activity Index score of 0.3 tweets at a rate per person 0.3 times as much as the continental average.
  • The Twitter Activity Index ranking

    • We intend to track changes in the Index over time, and produce work on the growth of social media over time – with a focus on African social media.
